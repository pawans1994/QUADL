<ul><li>Open Excel with a new worksheet.</li><li>First, we'll create a table with the given values:</li><li>To create this table, first make the row and column labels: pick a cell and type <code style="inline">Statistic</code>.  Then move one cell to the right and type <code style="inline">Value</code>.  </li><li>Now, in the cell underneath <code style="inline">Statistic</code>, type <code style="inline">Population Mean</code>.  In the cell to the right of that, enter the population mean (in this case, 507).  </li><li>Similarly, enter values for Population Standard Deviation and X.</li><li>Now we'll add two new statistics to the table we created: <code style="inline">z-score</code> and <code style="inline">P(X=700)</code> and calculate their values.</li><li>To calculate the z-score, we'll use the equation z = (X - mean)/stdev.  In this case, we get z = (700-507)/111, or 1.738739.</li><li>To calculate P(X=700), we'll use an Excel function called <code style="inline">NORMSDIST</code>, that takes a z-score and uses it to calculate a probability.  This step takes the place of us having to look up the probability in a normal table.  In the cell next to <code style="inline">P(X=700)</code>, type <code style="inline">=NORMSDIST(1.738739)</code>.  Excel tells us that the probability is 0.95896.</li></ul>
