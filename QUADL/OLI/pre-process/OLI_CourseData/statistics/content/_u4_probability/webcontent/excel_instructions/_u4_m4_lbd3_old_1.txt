<ul><li>Open Excel with a new worksheet.</li><li>To "create the fair die" in the worksheet, in the first 6 cells under column A enter 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, and in the first 6 cells under column B enter the formula <code style="inline">=1/6</code> which is the corresponding probability for each number of the die.</li><li>Choose <code style="inline">Tools -&gt; Data Analysis</code> from the menubar.  In the pop-up window, choose <code style="inline">Random Number Generation</code>.</li><li>In the next window, enter 2 next to <code style="inline">Number of Variables</code>.  Enter 1000 next to <code style="inline">Number of Random Numbers</code>.  Choose <code style="inline">Discrete</code> from the <code style="inline">Distribution</code> pull-down menu.  Enter 0.6 next to <code style="inline">p Value</code>.  Finally, enter A1:B6 next to <code style="inline">Value and Probability Input Range</code>.  Click OK.</li><li>Excel will create a new worksheet with the data.  </li></ul>
