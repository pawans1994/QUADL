<p>First we need to sort our data to make it easier to tabulate:</p><br><ul><li>Type Control-A twice to select all of the data in the worksheet.</li><li>Choose <code style="inline">Sort &amp; Filter -&gt; Custom Sort</code> from the <code style="inline">Editing</code> group on the <code style="inline">Home</code> tab.</li><li>In the pop-up window, make sure that <code style="inline">My data has headers</code> is selected and then choose <code style="inline">Treatment</code> from the pull-down menu next to <code style="inline">Sort by</code>.</li><li>Click the <code style="inline">Add Level</code> button and then choose <code style="inline">Gender_SRS</code> from the pull-down menu next to <code style="inline">Sort by</code> in the new row that was just added.  Click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li></ul><br><p>Now we'll create our two-way table.  First we'll make the row and column headings and then use excel formulas to get the counts:</p><br><ul><li>In an empty section of your worksheet, pick a cell and type <code style="inline">Statistic</code>.  Then move to the right one cell and type <code style="inline">Female</code>, move right and type <code style="inline">Male</code>, and move right and type <code style="inline">Total</code> for our column headings.</li><li>For the row headings, start in the cell under <code style="inline">Statistic</code> and type <code style="inline">Treatment 1</code>, then move down one cell and type <code style="inline">Treatment 2</code>, move down and type <code style="inline">Treatment 3</code>, and move down and type <code style="inline">Total</code>.</li><li>Now select the cell under <code style="inline">Female</code> and to the right of <code style="inline">Treatment 1</code>, and type <code style="inline">=counta([range])</code>, where [range] represents the females in the <code style="inline">Gender_SRS</code> column that have a Treatment value of 1.</li><li>Move one cell to the right (under <code style="inline">Male</code> and to the right of <code style="inline">Treatment 1</code>) and type <code style="inline">=counta([range])</code>, where [range] represents the males in the <code style="inline">Gender_SRS</code> column that have a Treatment value of 1..  Repeat this for each cell in the table, adjusting the data range appropriately.</li><li>Finally, compute the <code style="inline">Total</code> for each treatment by adding the numbers in the <code style="inline">Male</code> and <code style="inline">Female</code> columns.  The specific cell references depend on where exactly you created your two-way table, but if D2 represents the number of females in the first treatment group, and E2 represents the total number of males in the first treatment group, the formula would be <code style="inline">=D2+E2</code> .</li></ul>
