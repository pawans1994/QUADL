<ul><li>Right-click one of the cells in your table and choose <code style="inline">PivotChart</code> from the pop-up menu.</li><li>Excel will create a new chart with a bar graph.  We want to change this to a pie-chart:</li><li>Right-click somewhere in the graph <code style="inline">outside of the bar</code>.</li><li>Choose <code style="inline">Chart Type</code> from the pop-up menu.</li><li>Now select  <code style="inline">Pie</code> from the <code style="inline">Chart type</code> list on the left and then click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li><li>To complete the pie-chart, drag the <code style="inline">Handed</code> item from the <code style="inline">PivotTable Field List</code> to the legend where it says <code style="inline">Drop Category Fields Here</code>.  Note: if you don't see the legend, the PivotTable Field list window may be covering it.</li><li>To label the sections of the pie, right click one of the sections of the pie and choose <code style="inline">Format Data Series</code> from the pop-up menu, select the tab marked <code style="inline">Data Labels</code>, and then check the boxes next to <code style="inline">Value</code> and <code style="inline">Percentage</code> and click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li></ul>
