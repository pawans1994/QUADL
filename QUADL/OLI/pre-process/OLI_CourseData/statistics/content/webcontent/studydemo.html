Course Overview         

Overview:
This course is an online intro stats course, which is very similar to 36-201, the statistics course you have just completed. Today, you'll go over a very small fraction of the course: Describing the distribution of a quantitative variable with numbers. Watching you navigate through the course and interact with it will give us valuable feedback and will help us make future design decisions. We appreciate your cooperation.

Navigation:
You can use the right and left arrows in the navigation bar at the top or bottom of the screen to move from page to page in the course.

You can also use the navigation frame on the left to quickly move through course topics.

Learning Opportunities/Activities:
Sprinkled throughout the course are buttons that represent additional learning opportunities or activities:

  • Module Objectives - At any point during the course, from any page of the course, you can see the learning objectives for the current module by clicking on the objectives button in the top or bottom nav bar.

  • Learn By Doing - Periodically, you will be given opportunities to practice what you have learned. These activities typically contain instructions for performing an activity in Minitab, and then ask you questions about the activity. You can then compare your answers with answers that the course author would have given.

       Learn By Doing   

  • Did I Get This? - Throughout the course, there are opportunities for you to take self-assessments to gauge your understanding of the content. These self-assessments do not count towards your grade, and can be taken as many times as desired. These self-assessments will sometimes appear as a link and open in a separate window:

       Did I Get This?  

    and other times will appear in-line on the page you are reading (like an example):
    DID I GET THIS?

    (The question(s) will be here)

  • Many Students Wonder... - This button will launch a page that contains frequently asked questions. These questions generally address common misconceptions that students have, or clarify points that have been made in the main page.
