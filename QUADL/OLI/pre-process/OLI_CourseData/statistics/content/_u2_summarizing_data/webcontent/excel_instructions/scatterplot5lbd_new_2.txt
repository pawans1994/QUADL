This series currently includes all of the data points for both male and female.  We're going to change this series to represent only males, and add a new series to represent females.  To do this:</li><li>Click on the <code style="inline">weight</code> series to select it, and then click the <code style="inline">Edit</code> button.</li><li>In the box under <code style="inline">Series name</code>, type <code style="inline">males</code>.</li><li>In the box under <code style="inline">Series X values</code>, change the formula so that it the range includes only the data for males.  In this case, you would change it to <code style="inline">=Sheet1!$B$2:$B$58</code>.  Change the formula in the box under <code style="inline">Series Y values</code> in the same manner.  Click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li><li>Now click on the <code style="inline">Add</code> button to add a new series.</li><li>In the box under <code style="inline">Series name</code>, type <code style="inline">females</code>.</li><li>The easiest way to get the formulas right for the <code style="inline">Series X values</code> and <code style="inline">Series Y values</code> is to copy and paste the formulas from the male series.  Do that and change the data range appropriately.  In our case, for example, the formula for <code style="inline">Series X values</code> for the <code style="inline">females</code> series would be <code style="inline">=Sheet1!$B$59:$B$82</code>.</li><li>Click <code style="inline">OK</code> twice when you're all done.</li></ul>
