<ul><li> First we need to define the bins that Excel will use to create the histogram.  In this case, our minimum data point is 31, and our max is 76.  We'll use a bin width of 5, and make bins from 30 to 80:</li><li> In one of the columns near the data, enter these numbers: 30, 35, 40, 45, etc up to 80.</li><li> Now click the <code style="inline">Data Analysis</code> button in the <code style="inline">Analysis</code> group on the <code style="inline">Data</code> tab.  Then choose <code style="inline">Histogram</code> in the resulting pop-up window and click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li><li> In the next window, click in the box next to <code style="inline">Input Range</code> and then select the cells containing your data.</li><li> Then click in the box next to <code style="inline">Bin Range</code> and then select the cells containing your bin information.</li><li> Finally, check the box at the bottom of this window marked <code style="inline">Chart Output</code>, and then click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li><li> Excel will create a new worksheet containing the histogram and a table with the frequency counts.   The histogram is usually pretty small; you can click on it and then drag one of the handles on the outside to enlarge it.</li><li>Histograms shouldn't have spaces between the bars, so right-click on one of the bars and choose  <code style="inline">Format Data Series</code>.  Then set the gap width to 0% (No Gap).</li><li>Before closing the Format Data Series window, click on <code style="inline">Border Color</code> on the left, choose <code style="inline">Solid line</code>, and set the color to black.</li></ul>
