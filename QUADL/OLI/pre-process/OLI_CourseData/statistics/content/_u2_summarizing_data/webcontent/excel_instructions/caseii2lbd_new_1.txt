<p>First we need to sort our data to make it easier to tabulate:</p><ul><li>Type Control-A twice to select all of the data in the worksheet.</li><li>Choose <code style="inline">Sort & Filter -&gt; Custom Sort</code> from the <code style="inline">Editing</code> group in the <code style="inline">Home</code> tab.</li><li>In the pop-up window, make sure that <code style="inline">My data has headers</code> is checked and then choose <code style="inline">light</code> from the pull-down menu next to <code style="inline">Sort by</code>.</li><li>Click the <code style="inline">Add Level</code> button and then choose <code style="inline">nearsightedness</code> from the pull-down menu next to </code>Sort by</code> in the new row that was just added.  Click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li></ul><p>Now we'll create our two-way table.  First we'll make the row and column headings and then use excel formulas to get the counts:</p><ul><li>Pick a cell outside of the two columns of data, and type <code style="inline">Statistic</code>.  Then move to the right one cell and type <code style="inline">No</code>, move right and type <code style="inline">Yes</code>, and move right and type <code style="inline">Total</code> for our column headings.</li><li>For the row headings, start in the cell under <code style="inline">Statistic</code> and type <code style="inline">lamp</code>, then move down one cell and type <code style="inline">night light</code>, move down and type <code style="inline">no light</code>, and move down and type <code style="inline">Total</code>.</li><li>Now select the cell under <code style="inline">No</code> and to the right of <code style="inline">lamp</code>, and type <code style="inline">=counta(B2:B35)</code>.</li><li>Move one cell to the right (under <code style="inline">Yes</code> and to the right of <code style="inline">lamp</code>) and type <code style="inline">=counta(B36:B76)</code>.  Repeat this for each cell in the table, adjusting the data range appropriately.</li></ul>
