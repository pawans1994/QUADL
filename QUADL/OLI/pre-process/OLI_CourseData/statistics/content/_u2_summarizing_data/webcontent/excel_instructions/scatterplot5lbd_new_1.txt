<ul><li>Sort the data by gender:</li><li>Type Control-A twice to select all of the data in the worksheet.</li><li>Choose <code style="inline">Sort & Filter -&gt; Custom Sort</code> from the <code style="inline">Editing</code> group in the <code style="inline">Home</code> tab.</li><li>In the pop-up window, make sure that <code style="inline">My data has headers</code> is checked and then choose <code style="inline">gender</code> from the pull-down menu next to <code style="inline">Sort by</code>.  Click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li><li>Now select all of the data in columns B and C, and then choose <code style="inline">Scatter</code> in the <code style="inline">Charts</code> group on the <code style="inline">Insert</code> tab.</li><li>Choose the first scatterplot option (Scatter with only Markers).</li><li>Now we have a scatterplot, but the data is all on the right of the plot.  To fix this:</li><li>Right-click on the x-axis, and choose <code style="inline">Format Axis</code> from the pop-up menu.</li><li>Make sure that <code style="inline">Axis Options</code> is selected on the left, and then next to <code style="inline">Minimum</code> choose <code style="inline">Fixed</code> and enter <code style="inline">50</code> in the textbox.  Click <code style="inline">Close</code>.</li></ul>
