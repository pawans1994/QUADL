<ul><li>Right-click on one of the data points in your scatterplot and choose <code style="inline">Source Data</code> from the pop-up menu.</li><li>Click the tab labeled <code style="inline">Series</code>.</li><li>Right now only one series is shown, and it is labeled <code style="inline">weight</code>.  This series currently includes all of the data points for both male and female.  We're going to change this series to represent only males, and add a new series to represent females.  To do this:</li><li>In the box next to <code style="inline">Name</code>, type <code style="inline">males</code>.</li><li>In the box next to <code style="inline">X Values</code>, change the formula so that it the range includes only the data for males.  In this case, you would change it to <code style="inline">=Sheet1!$B$2:$B$58</code>.  Change the formula in the box next to <code style="inline">Y Values</code> in the same manner.</li><li>Now click on the <code style="inline">Add</code> button to add a new series.</li><li>In the box next to <code style="inline">Name</code>, type <code style="inline">females</code>.</li><li>The easiest way to get the formulas right for the <code style="inline">X Values</code> and <code style="inline">Y Values</code> is to copy and paste the formulas from the male series.  Do that and change the data range appropriately.  In our case, for example, the formula for <code style="inline">X values</code> for the <code style="inline">females</code> series would be <code style="inline">=Sheet1!$B$59:$B$82</code>.</li><li>Click <code style="inline">OK</code>.</li></ul>
