<ul><li>To create a scatterplot:</li><li>Select all of the data in columns A and B, and then choose <code style="inline">Scatter</code> in the <code style="inline">Charts</code> group on the <code style="inline">Insert</code> tab.</li><li>Choose the first scatterplot option (Scatter with only Markers).</li><li>Remember that if you like, you can adjust the axes to better see the data by right-clicking on an axis, choosing <code style="inline">Format Axis</code> from the pop-up menu, choosing <code style="inline">Axis Options</code> on the left in the window that comes up, and setting the minimum and maximum to something more appropriate than what Excel chose for you.</li><li>To compute the correlation:</li><li>Click in a cell outside of the first two columns of data.</li><li>Type <code style="inline">=correl([range1],[range2])</code> where [range1] is the first column of data and [range2] is the second column of data.</li></ul>
