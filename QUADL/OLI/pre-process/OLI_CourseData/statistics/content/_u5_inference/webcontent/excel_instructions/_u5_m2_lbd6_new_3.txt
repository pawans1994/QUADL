<p>Unfortunately, Excel can't create boxplots directly, so we have to create a column graph and alter it so that we get boxplots.  We start by using our Descriptive Statistics to create a new table that will be the basis for our column graph:</p><br><ul><li>Right below your Descriptive Statistics table, type <code style="inline">25th Percentile</code> .  Move down one cell and type <code style="inline">50th Percentile</code> , then underneath that <code style="inline">75th Percentile</code>, then <code style="inline">Lower limit</code> , and finally <code style="inline">Upper limit</code> .</li><li>The <code style="inline">25th Percentile</code> values are the same as those for Q1, so select the values for Q1, right-click and select <code style="inline">Copy</code>, and then select the empty cells in the <code style="inline">25th Percentile</code> row and right-click and select <code style="inline">Paste Special</code> .  In the pop-up window, choose <code style="inline">Values</code> and click <code style="inline">OK</code> .</li><li>The <code style="inline">50th Percentile</code> values are equal to <code style="inline">Median - Q1</code> , so enter the necessary formulas to calculate those values from the values in your Descriptive Statistics table.  For example, if the value for Blue Median is in cell M4, and the value for Blue Q1 is in cell M3, the correct formula for calculating the 50th Percentile for Blue would be <code style="inline">=M4-M3</code>.</li><li>The <code style="inline">75th Percentile</code> values are equal to <code style="inline">Q3 - Median</code>.</li><li>The <code style="inline">Lower limit</code> values are equal to <code style="inline">Q1 - min</code>.</li><li>The <code style="inline">Upper limit</code> values are equal to <code style="inline">Max - Q3</code>.</li></ul><br><p>Now we can finally create our boxplots:</p><br><ul><li>Highlight the values in the <code style="inline">25th Percentile</code> , <code style="inline">50th Percentile</code> , and <code style="inline">75th Percentile</code> rows, and then choose <code style="inline">Column</code> in the <code style="inline">Charts</code> group on the <code style="inline">Insert</code> tab.</li><li>In the pull-down menu, choose the second option under <code style="inline">2-D Column</code> (labeled <code style="inline">Stacked Column</code> if you hover your mouse over it).</li><li>Excel will create a stacked column graph for you.  The idea here is that the top two boxes in each column will make up our boxplots.  We'll add error bars for the min and Max values, and simply remove the bottom box.</li><li>Click on the top box in one of the columns to select it, and then choose <code style="inline">Error Bars -&gt; More Error Bars Options</code> in the <code style="inline">Analysis</code> group on the <code style="inline">Layout (Chart Tools)</code> tab.</li><li>In the pop-up window, choose <code style="inline">Vertical Error Bars</code> on the left and then choose <code style="inline">Plus</code> on the right.</li><li>Choose <code style="inline">Custom</code> under <code style="inline">Error Amount</code> and then click the <code style="inline">Specify Value</code> button.</li><li>In the pop-up window, in the <code style="inline">Positive Error Value</code> box, enter the range of values in the <code style="inline">Upper limit</code> row in the table we created (or click and drag to select those values).  Click <code style="inline">OK</code> and then <code style="inline">Close</code> .</li><li>Now repeat this error bar exercise for the bottom box in the columns, except choose <code style="inline">Minus</code> error bars, and enter the range of values in the <code style="inline">Lower limit</code> row of our table into the <code style="inline">Negative Error Value</code> box.</li><li>Right-click on the top box in one of the columns and choose <code style="inline">Format Data Series</code>.  In the pop-up window, choose <code style="inline">Fill</code> on the left, and specify <code style="inline">No fill</code>.  Then choose <code style="inline">Border Color</code> on the left and specify <code style="inline">Solid Line</code>.</li><li>Repeat for the middle box in the columns.</li><li>Repeat for the bottom box in the columns, except this time specify <code style="inline">No fill</code> and <code style="inline">No line</code>.  This will cause the bottom box to simply disappear.</li><li>Click on the legend and hit the Delete key to remove it.</li><li>Click on the horizontal lines in the background of the graph and hit the Delete key to remove them.</li><li>Finally, right-click on the y-axis, and choose <code style="inline">Format Axis</code> from the pop-up menu.  Make sure that <code style="inline">Axis Options</code> is selected on the left, and then next to <code style="inline">Minimum</code> choose <code style="inline">Fixed</code> and enter <code style="inline">20</code> in the textbox.  Click <code style="inline">Close</code>.</li></ul>
