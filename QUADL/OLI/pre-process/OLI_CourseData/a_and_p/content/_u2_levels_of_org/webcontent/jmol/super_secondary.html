Globular Proteins - Super-secondary Structures:
  • Individual a-helices and b-sheets are stabilzed by main-chain hydrogen bonds.
  • Helix-helix and helix-sheet interactions usually involve non-polar residues and are therefore stabilized by the hydrophobic effect and van der Waals.
  • b-barrels close the edge of b-sheets, satisfying H-bonds on edge of sheet.
  1. Single Domain Proteins:
    • Triose Phosphate Isomerase - b-barrel core, bab units make up the walls. a-helicies are on the outside.
    • Fatty Acid Binding Protein - b-barrel formed of anti-|| b-strands. Contains both a b-meander and an aa unit.
    • Prealbumin - b-barrel formed of a Greek key.
    • Protein G - single a packed against b-sheet. Contains several b-hairpins Note that the sheet is a mixture of || and anti-|| b-strands.

  2. Fibrous Proteins
    1. Coiled-Coil (Leucine Zipper)
      • Two 'normal' ahelices wound together in a 'super-helix'
      • Held together by hydrophobic interactions between Leucine residues
    2. Silk
      • || planes of anti-parallel b strands
      • Repeating unit is Ala-Gly.
      • Hydrophobic interface between each plane due to Ala methyl groups
    3. Collagen
      • Triple helix
      • Repeating unit: Gly-Pro-Pro
      • Large number of Proline residues are hydroxylated (requires vitamin C)
      • Gly residues are on the inside because of their small size
